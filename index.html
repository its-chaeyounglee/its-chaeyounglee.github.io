About Me

I am an undergraduate student majoring in Nursing at Seoul National University, where I am also pursuing interdisciplinary minors in Brain-Mind-Behavior and Learning Sciences, along with a Microdegree in Big Data.

With a strong interest in integrating digital technology with human-centered healthcare, I have participated in multiple research projects ranging from cognitive neuroscience studies on children’s memory and language development to deep learning for CEUS-based liver tumor classification.

Currently, I am a research intern at the AI Medical Imaging Lab, where I contribute to developing and evaluating deep learning models for medical image analysis. I am also leading the development of a Python-based self-assessment platform designed to enhance mental health awareness among university students.

My goal is to bridge clinical insight and data-driven innovation to create meaningful tools that improve people’s lives. I’m still learning, but I approach every new challenge with curiosity, humility, and a deep sense of responsibility.

In my free time, I love exploring cozy bookstores, enjoying musicals and art exhibitions, and reading everything from insightful non-fiction to comforting novels.
